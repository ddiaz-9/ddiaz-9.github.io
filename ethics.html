Information Technology Ethics

Within the field of information technology, individuals are to follow guidelines that consider various factors concerning the implementation, use, and impacts of technology. Specifically, the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) have several outlined standards that apply to all types of professions, ranging instructors to students on detailed principles and is noted as the ACM Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct (Association for Computing Machinery, 2018). Defined standards are seperated into four sections, describing ethical principles, responsibilities/duties, leadership/voluntary roles, and compliance, all of which guide on proper conduct. The first standard section, General Ethical Principles, involves all users of technolgy to contribute towards creating benefits that positively affect others and their surroundings. The act of providing benefits are heavily emphasized with the importance of avoiding harm in the form of consequences, physical injuries, and damage of information, leading to the need for honesty and considerations of others.
