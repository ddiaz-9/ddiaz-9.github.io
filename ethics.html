Information Technology Ethics

Within the field of information technology, individuals are to follow guidelines that consider various factors concerning the implementation, use, and impacts of technology. Specifically, the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) have several outlined standards that apply to all types of professions, ranging instructors to students on detailed principles and is noted as the ACM Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct (Association for Computing Machinery, 2018). Defined standards are seperated into four sections, describing ethical principles, responsibilities/duties, leadership/voluntary roles, and compliance, all of which guide on proper conduct.

The first standard section, General Ethical Principles, involves all users of technolgy to contribute towards creating benefits that positively affect others and their surroundings (Association for Computing Machinery, 2018). The act of providing benefits are heavily emphasized with the importance of avoiding harm in the form of consequences, physical injuries, and damage of information, leading to the need for honesty and considerations of others. Part of that includes attributing proper credit to the authors/creators of technologies and ideas where necessary, as individuals hold the right to their own works.

Professional responsibilities are additional considerations when tasked with working on technology alongside other users (Association for Computing Machinery, 2018). Notably, all of those involved may expect quality work between each other and maintain effective communication in order to uphold task objectives. Rules and policies in a different contexts such as national or organizational are necessary standards to be followed, as different rulings in place may pertain to order and handling of information.

Professional leadership principles details on individuals assigned leadership/influential positions to maintain considerations of colleagues and the public, all while fulfilling management responsibilities (Association for Computing Machinery, 2018). Enforcement of rules and compliance within an organization are continuous tasks to be performed, along with acting on violations that occur. Leaders are to oversee implementations of systems and ensure correct usage and careful modifications take place as many users rely on systems for performing operations and may affect productivity rates. While leadership standards especially pertain to leading roles, all technical users are intended to follow and fulfill the principles.

Compliance with the code is final significant principle associated with all users, calling for all previous standards to be complied with (Association for Computing Machinery, 2018). All individuals should attempt to create solutions for violations and communicating with those who are suspected to be noncompliant. Adherence is expected from all, including whether members are apart of the ACM or not.
